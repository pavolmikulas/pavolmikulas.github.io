I am cross-disciplinary designer, who believes that human-centered design can make our planet a better place to live. I believe in the power of positive thinking and concentration on user. I have a strong aesthetic sensibility, technical education and experience in manufacturing technologies. I am a design thinker for whom each project is searching for an answer to the right question.

In 2005 I became a member of Olgoj Chorchoj design studio where I was responsible for a number of projects, including the design for Olgoj Chorchoj’s 2005 Designblok exhibition, the Carafe and water glass design made by Moravian Glassworks Květná, the web code and design for Olgoj Chorchoj’s website, and the 2006 Design of Pilsner Urquell photo award trophy.

I attended a workshop on the Scuola Politecnica di Design in Milan in collaboration with Alessi under the direction of Marti Guixe and Jack Brandsma.

I served a six months internship at Volkswagen, Wolfsburg, Germany, in the department for research and development.

In 2012 I founded the design studio Vývojka. My clients include Škoda Auto, Prim, DZ Dražice, Slovenská sporiteľňa, Eset and Re pello.

I received Slovak National Design Prize for RePello Model 16, a wooden, full-suspension bicycle for children.

Since 2018, I have been working for TDK as an IC layout engineer. We develop high performance, low power digital MEMS microphones.

I live and work in Bratislava with my wife Eva and daughters Margareta and Milota.
